Project Aims

Aim 1

Optimize the preclinical MRI protocols to improve their robustness to respiratory motion-induced corruption, distortion and blur. Assess the repeatability of quantitative imaging markers in GEM model of pancreatic cancer.

Aim 2

Design and perform preclinical studies that match an ongoing clinical trial that evaluate the stroma-directed agent on pancreatic cancer microenvironment. Perform co-clinical imaging studies in human and GEM model.

Aim 3

Build a web resource to demonstrate the effective optimizations of the motion-robust methods, share protocols, tools and SOPs. Enhance the accessibility, clarity and sustainability of the webpage.

The Co-Clincal Imaging Resources Program (U24s)

The NCI Co-Clinical Imaging Research Resource Program (CIRP) promotes development of quantitative imaging resources for therapeutic or prevention co-clinical trials that study both patients and human-in-mouse models. The resources will encourage a consensus on optimizing quantitative imaging methods and develop standard operating procedures for co-clinical applications
